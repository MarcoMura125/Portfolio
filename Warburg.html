Derivation of the Electrochemical Impedance for Linear Semi-Infinite Diffusion

This section presents the derivation of a linearized model that describes the electrochemical impedance associated with charge transfer and mass transport phenomena occurring at faradaic electrodes. After completing the derivation, I combined this impedance model with additional effects (electrolyte resistance and double-layer capacitance) to obtain the Randles circuit, in which each element has a clear and well-defined physical meaning. Finally, I plotted the Nyquist diagram of the Randles equivalent circuit impedance, varying the concentrations of the redox species.

This work is largely based on the video series Mass Transport in EIS, published by the YouTube channel Electrochemical Technology, funded by the Linnemann Research Group based at Paderborn University, Germany.

My aim was to condense the derivation and to add some additional insight by plotting the Randles equivalent circuit impedance Nyquist diagram in MATLAB, experimenting with different parameter values.

Below are the downloadable files: a comprehensive presentation summarizing the derivation and a ZIP archive containing the MATLAB code used to plot the Nyquist diagram of the Randles equivalent circuit impedance.
